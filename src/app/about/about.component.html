Front-end and me


For the past 5 years I've been delving deeper and deeper into software development, with languages ranging from desktops C/C++/C#/Java over frontend Javascript/AngularJS all the way to backend PHP/SQL and I gotta say that I kinda stuck to Front-End since the evolution of the Internet is going wild and you can do so much more then you could a couple years ago.


Front-end web applications are on pair with regular desktop applications in the terms of execution and optimization, while being a lot more compatible over a wide range of devices.


If you're building a mobile app it's worth considering a mobile web aplication since, instead of building apps for 3 different platforms (Windows, Android, iOS) in 3 different languages, you can simply write a mobile web application using modern frameworks such as Ionic and Angular, saves a lot of time, money and works equally well on all devices.
